Our Heritage

A route
centuries
in the making.

The Dutch and the Cape have been intertwined since Jan van Riebeeck anchored in Table Bay in 1652. Dutch Cape Trading Co. is a modern chapter in that long story — rooted in respect for craft, culture, and the remarkable things people make when left to their work.

We believe commerce, when done right, is an act of cultural preservation. Every purchase keeps a maker in business and a tradition alive.

1652
Dutch East India Company establishes the Cape of Good Hope as a refreshment station, planting the first vines.
1994
South Africa's democratic transition opens new opportunity for artisan trade across the globe.
1998
Dutch Cape Trading Company founded in Amsterdam, first container of Cape wine arrives in Rotterdam.
2024
Celebrating 26 years — 48 maker partnerships, two continents, one purpose.

How It Works

From hand
to home.

01
🔍
We Source
Our team travels to estates, studios, and cooperatives. We taste, touch, and discuss directly with makers before any agreement is made.
→
02
🤝
We Partner
Fair, long-term contracts. Makers know what they'll receive and when. No fluctuating spot prices. No exploitation. Just honest trade.
→
03
⚓
We Ship
Consolidated sea freight, climate-controlled where needed. We offset every tonne of CO₂ through verified reforestation in the Western Cape.
→
04
🎁
We Deliver
To boutiques, hotels, restaurants, and private collectors across Europe. Every order packed by hand. Every package includes the maker's story.
